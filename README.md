# the-forest-of-knowledge

**About** (2026-05-06)

**“The Forest of Knowledge”** website was created to accompany the publication: 
**Cámara-Leret, R., Roehrdanz, P. & Bascompte, J (2026) The forest of knowledge under global change. _Nature_.**

The publication was a collaboration between Prof. Rodrigo Cámara-Leret (University of Zurich, Department of Systematic and Evolutionary Botany), Prof. Jordi Bascompte (University of Zurich, Department of Evolutionary Biology and Environmental Studies) and Dr. Patrick R. Roehrdanz (Conservation International). The publication was supported by the Swiss National Science Foundation, the University of Zurich, the National Science Foundation, and the John and Jody Arnhold UC Santa Barbara-Conservation International Climate Solutions Collaborative.

**“The Forest of Knowledge” website** contains bibliographic information compiled by Prof. Rodrigo Cámara-Leret (https://rcamaraleret.com) to understand the benefits that native plants provide to the inhabitants of all countries of the Amazon basin. It comprises 76,390 literature reports of 5,796 native species sourced from 700 references published between the 16th to 21st century. The Forest of Knowledge website is supported by a Swiss National Science Foundation Starting Grant “INDIGENOMICS” (Grant number: 211659) (https://data.snf.ch/grants/grant/211659).

**“The Forest of Knowledge” website version 1.0** (www.the-forest-of-knowledge.com) was developed by Nisha Marwaha (https://github.com/nmarwaha), a PhD student at the Cámara-Leret lab (https://www.systbot.uzh.ch/de/research/camaraleret-group.html).

**Data Downloads**
-	You can use this dataset freely and share it with your collaborators.
-	The data have been compiled from the literature and from the original publications whenever possible.
-	_The Forest of Knowledge_ website is not the author/owner of the data. It is only a way of sharing them with the community. 
- If you decide to use this dataset, you must: 
1. Cite the original paper: “Cámara-Leret, R., Roehrdanz, P. & Bascompte, J (2026) The forest of knowledge under global change. _Nature_.”
2. Cite this website: “This work used ‘_The Forest of Knowledge_’ dataset available in the website: www.the-forest-of-knowledge.com.” 
-	Use of The Forest of Knowledge website does not preclude citing the author(s) of individual studies when needed. All references are in the dataset file.
-	Use this dataset at your own risk. We are not responsible for the accuracy of the results derived from your research.
-	If you have any problems downloading the data, please let us know.
 
**How To Contribute**
-	If you have a study that you think could be included in the database, please send us an e-mail. We are also happy to hear about any potential mistakes or other feedback.
